Sofrito

Base Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 Bunches cilantro (stems included, chop off the very ends)
  • 1 Small red bell pepper
  • 1 Small green bell pepper
  • 1 Medium yellow onion
  • 1 Head garlic (peeled)
  • ~½ cup Olive oil (estimate; use only as much as needed to help blend)

Instructions

  1. 1 Prep vegetables: Roughly chop cilantro (stems included, just remove the very ends). Quarter the peppers and onion. Separate and peel the garlic cloves.
  2. 2
    Blend
    Add all vegetables to a food processor or blender. Drizzle a small amount of olive oil in first — just enough to coat the blades or the bottom of the container. Blend until smooth (or, per your texture preference). Add more oil in small drizzles only if the machine struggles to process the mixture.
  3. 3
    Freeze
    Pour the sofrito into ice cube trays and freeze until solid.
  4. 4
    Store
    Transfer frozen cubes to a freezer-safe plastic bag. Use 1 cube at a time in recipes as needed.
Prep Time: 20 min
Freeze Time: 4+ hours
Yield: ~24 ice cubes

Beans

Base Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 (16 oz) bag dried beans (white, small red, black, or your choice)
  • 1 Packet Goya Sazón (or a cleaner alternative)
  • 1 Whole yellow onion, peeled and halved
  • ½ Head garlic, peeled
  • 1 Bay leaf
  • - Dried parsley
  • - Dried oregano
  • - Ground cumin (optional; I like to include this for Black Beans)
  • - Black pepper, to taste
  • - Red pepper flakes, to taste

Instructions

  1. 1
    Soak
    Rinse beans well. Cover with water and soak overnight. Drain before cooking.
  2. 2
    Cook
    Add beans to a large pot and cover with filtered water (about 2 inches above beans).
  3. 3
    Season
    Add the Sazón packet, bay leaf,onion halves, garlic cloves, and spices. For parsley and oregano, lightly coat about 3/4 of the surface of the water with each seasoning… I have no idea how much that is - "let the ancestors speak to you".
  4. 4
    Simmer
    Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Cook until beans are tender, 1–2 hours depending on type. Add more water if needed during cooking.
  5. 5
    Remove
    Once beans are cooked, discard the bay leaf and onion halves. and keep as much, or little, of the now delicious, dissolving garlic cloves.
Soak Time: Overnight
Cook Time: 1-2 hours
Yield: ~6 cups

Frijoles Licuados

Progressive

Requires

  • 1 Sofrito from Step 1 (frozen cubes)
  • - Beans from Step 2 (with cooking broth)

Additional Ingredient

  • - Avocado oil (or other cooking oil of choice)

Instructions

  1. 1
    Blend
    In a blender, combine cooked beans with enough of their cooking broth to create a smooth, pourable mixture.
  2. 2
    Cook
    In a medium pot or skillet, heat a small amount of avocado oil over medium heat. Add 1 cube of the sofrito and cook, stirring occasionally, until fragrant.
  3. 3
    Mix
    Pour the blended beans into the pot with the sofrito. Stir well to combine.
  4. 4
    Simmer
    Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens slightly and flavors meld, about 5–10 minutes.
Prep Time: 5 min
Cook Time: 10 min
Yield: ~4 cups

Black Beans

Progressive

Requires

  • 1 Beans from Step 2 (black beans specifically)

Additional Ingredients

  • 1 Tablespoon ground cumin (recommended for black beans)
  • - Extra garlic cloves (optional, for more flavor)

Instructions

  1. 1 Follow the base Beans recipe, but use black beans specifically.
  2. 2
    Enhance
    Add extra cumin during cooking for enhanced flavor profile.
  3. 3
    Serve
    Black beans are perfect for rice dishes, soups, or as a side dish.
Cook Time: 1-2 hours
Yield: ~6 cups

Gallo Pinto

Progressive

Requires

  • 1 Sofrito from Step 1 (frozen cubes)
  • 1 Black Beans from Step 3 (with cooking broth)

Additional Ingredients

  • 2 cups of Rice (Uncle Ben's recommended)
  • 3 cups of bean soup
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1.5 cups of Beans
  • - Half of Stop & Shop Fritata Veggie Mix (red onions, yellow peppers, red peppers, green peppers)
  • - Or: 1 large red onion, 1 small yellow pepper, 1 small red pepper, 1 small green pepper
  • 5 tablespoons butter (eyeball this)
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar (or regular vinegar)
  • 1 cube Sofrito
  • 1 red packet Sazon
  • - Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. 1
    Prep
    Clean rice, dice veggies, defrost sofrito (or use fresh)
  2. 2
    Saute
    Use about 3 tablespoons of butter to saute the veggies til transparent, add sofrito, add beans, add rice (use a little bit of the cup of water to get it all out of the bowl), add sazon, mix together well
  3. 3
    Liquid
    Add the rest of the cup of water, add bean soup, bring to a boil
  4. 4
    Simmer
    Once boiling, add rice vinegar, cover and lower to medium-low
  5. 5
    Cook
    Don't touch it, don't look at it, pray to Baby Jesus and cook for 20 minutes
Prep Time: 15 min
Cook Time: 25 min
Yield: ~6 servings
